CLINICAL DRUG REFERENCE — SYNTHETIC (for development only)

=== PARACETAMOL (Acetaminophen) ===
Indications: Mild to moderate pain; fever reduction.
Standard Adult Dose: 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours. Maximum 4 g/day.
Contraindications: Severe hepatic impairment. Avoid in active alcohol use disorder.
Key Interactions: Warfarin (increases anticoagulant effect at high doses). Rifampicin (reduces plasma levels).
Monitoring: LFTs for long-term use. Overdose risk is dose-dependent; N-acetylcysteine is the antidote.

=== AMOXICILLIN ===
Indications: Bacterial infections — respiratory tract, urinary tract, skin, otitis media.
Standard Adult Dose: 250–500 mg three times daily (oral). Severe infections: 500 mg–1 g TDS.
Contraindications: Penicillin hypersensitivity. Use with caution in mononucleosis (risk of rash).
Key Interactions: Methotrexate (increased toxicity). Anticoagulants (monitor INR).
Monitoring: Renal function in prolonged therapy. Signs of Clostridium difficile colitis.

=== METFORMIN ===
Indications: Type 2 diabetes mellitus. First-line agent alongside diet modification.
Standard Adult Dose: 500 mg twice daily initially; increase to max 2–3 g/day in divided doses.
Contraindications: eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73 m². Iodinated contrast procedures (hold 48 h before/after).
Key Interactions: Alcohol (lactic acidosis risk). Cimetidine (increases metformin plasma levels).
Monitoring: eGFR at baseline and annually. Vitamin B12 every 2–3 years (depletion risk).

=== ATORVASTATIN ===
Indications: Hypercholesterolaemia; primary and secondary cardiovascular prevention.
Standard Adult Dose: 10–80 mg once daily (evening preferred for lipid synthesis rhythm).
Contraindications: Active liver disease. Pregnancy and breastfeeding.
Key Interactions: Clarithromycin / erythromycin (increase statin levels, myopathy risk). Fusidic acid (avoid combination).
Monitoring: LFTs before starting and if symptomatic. CK if myalgia develops.

=== SALBUTAMOL (Albuterol) ===
Indications: Acute bronchospasm in asthma and COPD. Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction.
Standard Adult Dose (inhaler): 100–200 mcg (1–2 puffs) as needed, up to 4 times daily.
Nebuliser: 2.5 mg in 2.5 mL saline, may repeat every 20 min in acute setting.
Contraindications: Hypersensitivity to salbutamol or excipients.
Key Interactions: Non-selective beta-blockers (antagonise bronchodilation). Loop diuretics (hypokalaemia risk with high doses).
Monitoring: Heart rate, tremor, potassium in acute/high-dose use.

=== WARFARIN ===
Indications: VTE treatment and prophylaxis; AF stroke prevention; mechanical heart valves.
Standard Adult Dose: Individualised — target INR 2–3 (most indications), 2.5–3.5 (mechanical valves).
Contraindications: Active major bleeding. Pregnancy (teratogen). Recent CNS/eye surgery.
Key Interactions: Numerous — any medication change requires INR monitoring. High risk with azole antifungals, amiodarone, NSAIDs.
Monitoring: INR. Weekly until stable, then monthly.

=== CLINICAL RED FLAGS (chest pain) ===
Immediate referral criteria:
- Crushing, central chest pain radiating to jaw/left arm: suspect ACS — activate emergency protocol.
- Pleuritic chest pain + dyspnoea + recent immobility: suspect PE — D-dimer / CTPA pathway.
- Tearing interscapular pain + hypertension: suspect aortic dissection — immediate imaging.
- Fever + productive cough + focal chest signs: pneumonia — assess severity (CURB-65).
