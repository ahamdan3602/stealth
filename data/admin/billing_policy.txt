ADMINISTRATIVE BILLING & COMPLIANCE POLICY — SYNTHETIC (for development only)

=== SECTION 1: BILLING CODE STANDARDS ===
All clinical encounters must be coded within 72 hours of the visit.
Primary diagnosis codes must follow ICD-10-CM. Secondary codes are required when they influence billing.
Upcoding or undercoding is a compliance violation subject to internal audit and corrective action.
Revenue cycle staff must complete annual coding certification renewal by Q1 of each year.

=== SECTION 2: INSURANCE VERIFICATION ===
Pre-authorisation is required for:
- Elective surgical procedures
- Advanced imaging (MRI, PET, CT beyond standard protocol)
- Specialist referrals outside the approved network
- Durable medical equipment over $500

Verification must be completed before the day of service. Failure to obtain authorisation results in a claim denial risk and is recorded in the staff performance log.

=== SECTION 3: PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY ===
Co-pays are collected at the time of service. No exceptions without Director approval.
Self-pay discount: 20% if payment is made in full at time of service; 10% on payment plans.
Charity care applications must be submitted with income verification within 30 days of the first bill.
Accounts over 90 days outstanding are referred to the external collections agency unless a payment plan is active.

=== SECTION 4: CLAIM SUBMISSION TIMELINES ===
Primary claims: submit within 30 days of service date.
Secondary claims: submit within 30 days of primary EOB receipt.
Appeals: submit within 60 days of denial date. Appeals require clinical documentation from the treating provider.

=== SECTION 5: HIPAA BILLING COMPLIANCE ===
Billing staff may access only the minimum necessary clinical information required for coding.
Accessing patient records beyond billing scope is a HIPAA violation and grounds for immediate suspension.
All billing communications (statements, EOBs) must be sent to the patient's address of record, not a third party, unless an authorised release is on file.

=== SECTION 6: INTERNAL AUDIT SCHEDULE ===
Random billing audits are conducted quarterly. A 5% sample of claims per department is reviewed.
Audit findings are shared with department heads within 10 business days.
Recoupment from overpaid claims is processed within the same billing cycle as discovery.
